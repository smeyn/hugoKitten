The research of **Machinery** is a mid-early game scientific milestone unlocked by **Writing**, and its primary purpose is to introduce **automation** and **industrial production** to your civilization.

It is considered a specialist technology, costing **15,000 Science**.

Here is a breakdown of what Machinery unlocks and its strategic importance:

### 1. Key Building: Steamworks

The most significant unlock is the **Steamworks** building.

*   **Function:** Steamworks consume Coal in exchange for creating a variety of handy items. It provides +1 Energy.
*   **Requirements:** Steamworks are themselves a hurdle, requiring **Steel**, **Gears**, and a **Blueprint** to create.

### 2. Key Upgrades for Automation and Production

Machinery unlocks three important upgrades in the Workshop tab:

| Upgrade | Purpose | Detail |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Printing Press** | **Manuscript Automation** | Allows Steamworks to automatically print **Manuscripts** (at a rate of 0.0025 manuscripts/sec). Manuscripts are required for subsequent advanced technologies like Theology and Astronomy. |
| **Workshop Automation** | **Resource Conversion** | Converts a percentage of wood (to beams) and minerals (to slabs) once per year when those resources are at 98% of their storage cap. |
| **Crossbow** | **Catpower Boost** | Provides a permanent **25% boost to the catpower production** generated by Hunters. |

### 3. Strategic Context

Machinery is part of a trio of important technologies unlocked by Writing (alongside **Philosophy** and **Steel**).

*   **Expert Advice:** One set of advice suggests prioritizing **Steel** first (to gain useful economic upgrades), then immediately moving to **Machinery** to utilize the hunting upgrade and gain automatic manuscript production, and finally researching **Philosophy**.

However, utilizing the Printing Press enabled by Machinery is key to overcoming the future hurdle of generating Manuscripts for Theology.
