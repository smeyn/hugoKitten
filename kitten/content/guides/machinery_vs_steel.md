The choice between researching [Machinery]( {{< relref "techs/machinery.md" >}}) versus [Steel]( {{< relref "techs/Steel.md" >}}) in *Kittens Game* involves prioritizing different aspects of technological advancement and economic development during the mid-game phase, after researching [Writing]( {{< relref "techs/Writing.md" >}}).

Machinery and Steel are considered "specialist Techs that wonâ€™t help you much right now" when Philosophy is the immediate priority to continue down the path of new science Techs like Theology. However, both are important for long-term growth.

Here is a comparison of the two technologies based on the sources:

### Steel Technology

*   **Cost and Prerequisite:** Steel costs **12,000 Science** and is unlocked by [Writing]( {{< relref "techs/Writing.md" >}}).
*   **Effect/Unlocks:**
    *   It **enables the resource Steel**, which is crafted from 100 Iron and 100 Coal.
    *   It unlocks several key **Workshop Upgrades**, including:
        *   [Steel Axe]( {{< relref "upgrade/Steel_Axe.md" >}}) (50% more effective Woodcutters).
        *   [Reinforced Warehouses]( {{< relref "upgrade/Reinforced_Warehouses.md" >}}) (25% more storage for various resources).
        *   [Coal Furnace]( {{< relref "upgrade/Coal_Furnace.md" >}}) (Smelters produce 0.005 coal/tick while burning wood).
        *   [Deep Mining]( {{< relref "upgrade/Deep_Mining.md" >}}) (Mines produce 0.003 coal/tick).
        *   [High Pressure Engine]( {{< relref "upgrade/High_Pressure_Engine.md" >}}) (reduces Steamworks coal consumption by 20%).
        *   [Steel Armour]( {{< relref "upgrade/Steel_Armour.md" >}}) (Hunting bonus +0.5).
*   **Strategic Importance:** A general strategy suggests starting with [Steel]( {{< relref "techs/steel.md" >}}) to get some useful upgrades that make your economy stronger. Acquiring Steel allows you to craft [Steel]( {{< relref "techs/steel.md" >}}), a composite part needed for Gears and complex machinery. You will need Steel later for construction, such as building Lumber Mills (which require Iron, and are needed to increase Wood output).

### Machinery Technology

*   **Cost and Prerequisite:** Machinery costs **15,000 Science** and is unlocked by [Writing]( {{< relref "techs/Writing.md" >}}).
*   **Effect/Unlocks:**
    *   It **unlocks the Steamworks building**.
    *   It unlocks Workshop Upgrades, including:
        *   [Printing Press]( {{< relref "upgrade/Printing_Press.md" >}}) (Steamworks automatically prints manuscripts).
        *   [Workshop Automation]( {{< relref "upgrade/Workshop_Automation.md" >}}) (Steamworks converts small quantities of wood/minerals to beams/slabs annually if resource is near cap).
        *   [Crossbow]( {{< relref "upgrade/Crossbow.md" >}}) (25% boost to catpower production from Hunters).
*   **Strategic Importance:** Machinery is valuable because it unlocks [Steamworks]( {{< relref "buildings/Steamworks.md" >}}). Steamworks consumes Coal to produce various handy items, including Manuscripts via the [Printing Press]( {{< relref "upgrade/Printing_Press.md" >}}) upgrade. Acquiring Machinery allows for a way to automatically make manuscripts, and provides a hunting upgrade.

### Strategic Takeaways

1.  **Prioritization:** It is suggested to research [Steel]( {{< relref "techs/steel.md" >}}) first to boost economic efficiency via storage and production upgrades, then [Machinery]( {{< relref "techs/machinery.md" >}}) for automation and manuscript generation, before finally tackling [Philosophy]( {{< relref "science/Philosophy.md" >}}) for Temples and Theology.
2.  **Manufacturing:** Steel is a necessary material for creating [Gears]( {{< relref "craft/Gear.md" >}}). Gears, along with Steel and a Blueprint, are required to build a [Steamworks]( {{< relref "buildings/Steamworks.md" >}}), which is unlocked by Machinery.
3.  [Manuscripts:]( {{< relref "craft/Manuscript.md" >}}) While Philosophy leads to Theology (which requires Manuscripts), Steamworks (unlocked by Machinery) can automate manuscript production via the Printing Press upgrade.

In essence, [Steel]( {{< relref "techs/Steel.md" >}}) provides necessary structural components and early resource bonuses, while [Machinery]( {{< relref "techs/machinery.md" >}}) unlocks advanced automation and specialized buildings (Steamworks).
