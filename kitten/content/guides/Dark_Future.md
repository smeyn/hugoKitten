[Dark Future]( {{< relref "guides/Dark_Future.md" >}}) is a specific game state in *Kittens Game* that triggers once you reach **Year 40,000** in the game calendar. It introduces a mix of penalties and powerful bonuses, marking a shift in endgame strategy.

### **Trigger Condition**
*   **Requirement:** You enter the Dark Future automatically when the game year reaches **40,000**.
*   **Time Skips:** This includes years gained artificially by shattering Time Crystals using the Chronoforge. You do not need to wait 40,000 real-time years; advanced players reach this state by skipping thousands of years instantly.

### **Effects**

#### **1. The Temporal Penalty (Negative)**
Upon entering the Dark Future, the fabric of time becomes unstable, making time travel more expensive.
*   **Increasing Costs:** The resource cost to shatter Time Crystals (and potentially other time mechanics) begins to increase the further you progress past year 40,000.
*   **Mitigation (Time Impedance):** You can counter this penalty by purchasing **Time Impedance** in the Time tab. Each level of Time Impedance suppresses the Dark Future penalty for **1,000 years**, effectively delaying the cost increase.

#### **2. Burned Paragon Buff (Positive)**
This is the primary strategic reason players aim for the Dark Future.
*   **Storage Boost:** Before the Dark Future, **Burned Paragon** provides a relatively small storage bonus (0.05% per point). Once Dark Future activates, this bonus quadruples to **0.2% per point**.
*   **Endgame Scaling:** This massive boost to global storage capacity allows you to hold significantly more Unobtainium, which is required to build the **70–100 Chronospheres** needed for infinite resource loops.

#### **3. Hawking Radiation Boost (Positive)**
*   **HR Harvesters:** These space structures generate energy based on black hole evaporation. In the Dark Future, their energy production increases based on how many years have passed since year 40,000.

### **Strategic Summary**
Reaching the Dark Future is a goal for "deep endgame" runs. Players purposefully shatter Time Crystals to reach year 40,000 to activate the **Burned Paragon bonus**, which provides the storage necessary to buy more [Chronospheres]( {{< relref "buildings/Chronosphere.md" >}}) and **Event Horizons**. They then manage the rising costs by purchasing **Time Impedance**.
