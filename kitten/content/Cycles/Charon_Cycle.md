The [Charon cycle]( {{< relref "Cycles/Charon_Cycle.md" >}}) is the first in the sequence of ten five-year cycles within the *Kittens Game* time system. Each cycle is associated with a specific glyph and provides production modifiers or festival bonuses once certain late-game Metaphysics technologies are researched.

### **Cycle Mechanics and Effects**
The effects of the Charon cycle are only active after researching specific Metaphysics technologies:

*   **Numerology Effects:** Researching **Numerology** activates modifiers for space-based structures during this cycle:
    *   [Lunar Outposts:]( {{< relref "planet/lunar_outpost.md" >}}) Unobtainium output is reduced by **10%**.
    *   **Entanglement Stations:** Gigaflops consumption is increased by **100%**.
*   **Numeromancy (Festival) Effects:** If [Numeromancy]( {{< relref "metaphysics/numeromancy.md" >}}) is researched, holding a **festival** during the Charon cycle provides a **1.5x multiplier** to the income of three basic resources: **Catnip, wood, and minerals**.
*   **Visual Indicator:** The cycle is represented by the glyph **⍙**. The color of this glyph changes annually to indicate progress through the five-year period: red (Year 0), orange-yellow (Year 1), green (Year 2), blue (Year 3), and violet (Year 4).

### **Relationship to Space Exploration**
The cycle is named after the **planet Charon**, which is the destination of a high-tier late-game space mission.
*   [Charon Mission:]( {{< relref "space/Charon_Mission.md" >}}) Unlocked after the Umbra mission, it requires **75,000 starcharts, 750,000 science, 35,000 kerosene, and 35,000 thorium**.
*   **Planet Charon:** Described as an incredibly dense dwarf planet at the edge of the Helios system, sometimes called "The Gates of Death".
*   **Entanglement Station:** Once Charon is reached, you can construct Entanglement Stations, which generate **hashes** for reverse-engineering relics but consume significant [energy]( {{< relref "resource/energy.md" >}}) and **gigaflops**. As noted above, their gigaflops consumption is doubled during the Charon cycle.

### **Strategic Context**
In the late game, the Charon cycle is generally considered a **"fallow" period** for industrial production, particularly because of the penalty to unobtainium output from Lunar Outposts. Advanced strategies for farming unobtainium often involve using a [Chronoforge]( {{< relref "upgrade/Chronoforge.md" >}}) to skip through the Charon and Umbra cycles to reach more profitable years, such as those in the [Redmoon cycle]( {{< relref "Cycles/Redmoon_cycle.md" >}}). Conversely, if a player is still in the early-to-mid game and needs to build up stocks of wood or minerals, the **Numeromancy festival bonus** makes Charon a useful time to run festivals.
