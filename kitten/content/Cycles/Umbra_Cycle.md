The [Umbra cycle]( {{< relref "Cycles/Umbra_Cycle.md" >}}) is the second of ten five-year cycles in the *Kittens Game* time system. Represented by the glyph **⍦**, it begins at Year 5 of every millennium and repeats every 50 years (Years 5–9, 55–59, etc.).

### **Cycle Mechanics and Modifiers**
The effects of the Umbra cycle are only active once specific late-game Metaphysics technologies have been researched:

*   **Numerology Effects:** This technology activates modifiers for space-based structures, primarily those located on the planet Dune and the Umbra black hole:
    *   **HR Harvester (Umbra):** Energy production is increased by **+50%**.
    *   **Hydraulic Fracturer (Dune):** Oil output is reduced by **-25%**.
    *   **Planet Cracker (Dune):** Uranium output is reduced by **-10%**.
*   **Numeromancy (Festival) Effects:** If a festival is held during this cycle and Numeromancy is researched, your village receives a **1.5x income multiplier** for four critical industrial resources: **Coal, iron, titanium, and gold**.

### **Relationship to Space Exploration**
The cycle is tied to the [Umbra Mission]( {{< relref "space/Umbra_Mission.md" >}}), which involves reaching a supermassive black hole at the heart of the Helios system.
*   **Mission Requirements:** Reaching Umbra requires **25,000 starcharts, 500,000 science, 25,000 kerosene, and 15,000 thorium**.
*   **HR Harvester:** Once reached, you can build HR Harvesters, which generate energy from black hole evaporation. These structures are significantly more efficient during the Umbra cycle.

### **Strategic Context**
*   **Energy Maximisation:** In the deep late game, players often rely on **HR Harvesters** for power. Because these receive a **50% boost** during the Umbra cycle, it is the most stable time for energy-heavy operations, such as running high numbers of [AI Cores]( {{< relref "buildings/AI_Core.md" >}}) or **Entanglement Stations**.
*   **Fallow Industrial Period:** For players farming [Unobtainium]( {{< relref "resource/unobtainium.md" >}}), the Umbra cycle is generally considered a "fallow" or less profitable period. Advanced players often use a [Chronoforge]( {{< relref "upgrade/Chronoforge.md" >}}) to skip through both the Charon and Umbra cycles to reach the more lucrative [Redmoon cycle]( {{< relref "Cycles/Redmoon_cycle.md" >}}), which provides massive bonuses to unobtainium production.
*   **Metal Stockpiling:** Despite being a "skip" year for space minerals, the **1.5x festival bonus** to basic metals makes it a useful time for mid-game players to refill their stocks of titanium and gold for terrestrial building projects.
