[Thorium]( {{< relref "resource/thorium.md" >}}) is a late-game craftable resource and fuel type in *Kittens Game*, described as a highly radioactive and unstable material. It is essential for deep-space exploration and enhancing the efficiency of your industrial energy grid.

### **Technological Unlock and Costs**
*   **Technology:** You must research the [Thorium]( {{< relref "resource/thorium.md" >}}) science technology, which becomes available after [Orbital Engineering]( {{< relref "techs/Orbital_Engineering.md" >}}).
*   **Research Cost:** 375,000 Science and **375 Blueprints**.
*   **Unlocked Upgrades:** Researching this technology enables the thorium resource and unlocks the [Thorium Reactors]( {{< relref "upgrade/Thorium_Reactors.md" >}}), [Thorium Drive]( {{< relref "upgrade/Thorium_Drive.md" >}}), and [Quantum Dot Cells]( {{< relref "upgrade/Quantum_Dot_Cells.md" >}}) workshop upgrades.

### **Acquisition and Production**
There are two primary ways to obtain Thorium once the technology is researched:
*   **Workshop Crafting:** You can craft thorium from [Uranium]( {{< relref "resource/uranium.md" >}}).
    *   **Recipe:** 250 Uranium yields 1 Thorium.
    *   **Efficiency:** Kittens with the **Chemist** trait provide a specific crafting bonus (+0.075) to thorium yield.
*   [Trading:]( {{< relref "guides/trading.md" >}}) Once you have reached a **Level 5 Embassy** with the [Dragons]( {{< relref "Civilisations/Dragons.md" >}}), you can trade with them for thorium.
    *   **Exchange:** They buy 250 Titanium and have a **50% chance** to sell 1 unit of Thorium (with a 25% range).

### **Key Workshop Upgrades**
Thorium is a required input for several high-tier upgrades that optimize your village and space travel:
*   [Thorium Reactors:]( {{< relref "upgrade/Thorium_Reactors.md" >}})
    *   **Effect:** Increases the energy output of your **Reactors by 25%** (+2.5 Watts).
    *   **Upkeep:** While active, each Reactor consumes **-0.05 thorium per tick**. 
    *   **Strategy:** Players are advised to **turn off** this upgrade after purchasing and only activate it when time-skipping to generate antimatter, as the thorium drain can quickly deplete stockpiles.
*   [Enriched Thorium:]( {{< relref "upgrade/Enriched_Thorium.md" >}}) Costs 500,000 Thorium; it **reduces Reactor thorium consumption by 25%**.
*   [Thorium Drive:]( {{< relref "upgrade/Thorium_Drive.md" >}}) Costs 100,000 Thorium; it significantly increases space travel speed, **dividing travel times by 50**.
*   [Quantum Dot Cells:]( {{< relref "upgrade/Quantum_Dot_Cells.md" >}}) Costs 1,000 Thorium; it improves [Solar Farm]( {{< relref "buildings/Solar_Farm.md" >}}) efficiency throughout the year, particularly providing a 30% boost in spring and autumn.

### **Space Missions and Infrastructure**
Thorium is a major mission-gate resource for reaching the farthest points of the Helios system:
*   [Umbra Mission:]( {{< relref "space/Umbra_Mission.md" >}}) 15,000 Thorium.
*   [Charon Mission:]( {{< relref "space/Charon_Mission.md" >}}) 35,000 Thorium.
*   [Centaurus System Mission:]( {{< relref "space/Centaurus_System_Mission.md" >}}) 50,000 Thorium.
*   **Furthest Ring Mission:** 75,000 Thorium.
*   **Tectonic (Centaurus structure):** Requires 75,000 Thorium to build.

### **Endgame Strategic Utility**
In the late-game "Iron Will" 40k runs, thorium becomes a critical bottleneck for reaching high antimatter caps. To reach a **5,000 Antimatter capacity** for relic stations, players often need around 34 Heatsinks, which carries a total cost of approximately **526,000 Thorium**. Because of these massive costs, managing your uranium-to-thorium crafting chain is vital for entering the Relic and Antimatter eras.
