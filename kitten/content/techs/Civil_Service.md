**Civil Service** is an early-game science technology that focuses on village management and kitten specialization. It is essential for transitioning from a basic settlement to a more organized and efficient civilization.

### **Acquisition and Costs**
*   **Science Cost:** 1,500 Science.
*   **Prerequisites:** You must first research **Animal Husbandry**.
*   **Unlocks:** Researching Civil Service provides the path to **Currency** and enables the **Census** feature.

### **The Census Feature**
Unlocking Civil Service enables the **Census** section within the Village tab, providing detailed information about your population.
*   **Kitten Identity:** It reveals the names of your individual kittens (e.g., Lily, Oscar, Tails, Shadow). 
*   **Performance Monitoring:** You can view how ably each kitten performs in their respective jobs.
*   **Skill Levels:** The Census tracks job experience, showing skill tiers such as Dabbling, Novice, Competent, and Master. These tiers provide multiplicative productivity bonuses for that specific job, ranging from **1%** at Novice to **18.75%** at Master.

### **Leader System**
Civil Service grants the ability to assign a single kitten as the **Leader** of the village.
*   **Assigning a Leader:** A kitten can be made Leader by clicking the star icon next to their name in the Census.
*   **Leader Traits:** Each kitten is born with a random trait (e.g., Scientist, Artisan, Merchant) that provides a specific bonus only when they are assigned as the leader. For example, an **Artisan** leader increases your global `craftRatio` by **+0.05**, while a **Scientist** reduces the science cost of technologies by **5%**.
*   **Promotion and Rank:** Leaders can be **Promoted** using gold and accumulated experience (Exp). Higher ranks significantly boost the kitten's job production, and if you research the **Republic** policy, the leader's rank provides a small production bonus to all other kittens in the village.

### **Strategic Importance**
In the early game, Civil Service is primarily used to identify and assign kittens to jobs they are most skilled in, ensuring maximum resource yield. By promoting a leader who is highly skilled in a bottlenecked resource (like Woodcutting), you can effectively gain the equivalent of several extra kittens gathering that resource. Additionally, beelining this technology is required to unlock **Currency**, which is vital for starting international trade.
