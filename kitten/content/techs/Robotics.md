[Robotics]( {{< relref "techs/Robotics.md" >}}) is a pivotal late-mid-game science in *Kittens Game*, marking a critical transition in the "Prestige and Energy Transition Epoch". It focuses on industrial automation, significant energy infrastructure shifts, and reducing the biological burden of your kitten workforce.

### **Costs and Prerequisites**
*   **Science Cost:** 140,000.
*   **Resource Cost:** 80 Blueprints.
*   **Prerequisite:** You must have researched [Electronics]( {{< relref "techs/Electronics.md" >}}).

### **The Hydro Plant Pivot**
Researching Robotics allows you to upgrade your [Aqueducts]( {{< relref "buildings/Aqueduct.md" >}}) into [Hydro Plants]( {{< relref "buildings/Hydro_Plant.md" >}}).
*   **Effect:** Each Hydro Plant provides **+5 Energy** (which can be increased to 5.75Wt with the Hydro Plant Turbines upgrade).
*   **Strategic Risk:** This is described as a "high-risk" infrastructure pivot. While Hydro Plants are vital for powering your growing industrial base, upgrading them causes you to lose the **+3% catnip production bonus** provided by Aqueducts.
*   **Recommendation:** Do not upgrade until your titanium production is robust and you are certain your catnip production (boosted by Paragon and Karma) can sustain the population without the Aqueduct bonus.

### **Unlocked Workshop Upgrades**
Robotics provides access to several upgrades that drastically improve industrial output and kitten efficiency:
*   [Robotic Assistance:]( {{< relref "upgrade/Robotic_Assistance.md" >}}) Reduces the catnip consumption of working kittens by **25%**. This is essential for managing the "happiness liability," where high happiness levels normally cause massive spikes in food demand.
*   [Steel Plants:]( {{< relref "upgrade/Steel_Plants.md" >}}) This causes [Calciners]( {{< relref "buildings/Calciner.md" >}}) to automatically convert 10% of their iron output into [Steel]( {{< relref "craft/Steel.md" >}}) (at a 100:1 ratio).
*   [Rotary Kiln:]( {{< relref "upgrade/Rotary_Kiln.md" >}}) Dramatically enhances Calciner efficiency, making them **75% more effective at producing Iron** and **3 times as effective at producing Titanium**.
*   [Factory Robotics:]( {{< relref "upgrade/Factory_Robotics.md" >}}) Improves [Engineer]( {{< relref "job/Engineer.md" >}}) effectiveness by increasing the "know-how" of Class 1, 2, and 3 Engineers.

### **Resource and Progression Unlocks**
*   [Tankers:]( {{< relref "craft/Tanker.md" >}}) Robotics enables the crafting of [Tankers]( {{< relref "craft/Tanker.md" >}}) in the Workshop. Each Tanker increases your maximum **Oil capacity by 500**. While useful for reaching the high oil caps required for space missions, some strategies suggest prioritizing steel-grinding for [Oil Wells]( {{< relref "buildings/Oil_Well.md" >}}) instead, as Tankers can be expensive in the early-space era.
*   [Artificial Intelligence:]( {{< relref "techs/Artificial_Intelligence.md" >}}) Robotics is the mandatory prerequisite for the [Artificial Intelligence]( {{< relref "techs/Artificial_Intelligence.md" >}}) science, which leads to AI Cores and the Gigaflop era.

### **Strategic Significance**
Robotics represents the point where your civilization begins to "replace" marginal agricultural bonuses with **high-density energy generation** and automated manufacturing. By reducing food demand and automating steel and titanium production, Robotics allows you to focus on the massive resource requirements of the **Space era** and the transition toward [Antimatter]( {{< relref "resource/Antimatter.md" >}}).
