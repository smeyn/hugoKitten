[Calendar]( {{< relref "techs/Calendar.md" >}}) is the foundational science technology in *Kittens Game*, costing **30 Science** to research. It is the primary prerequisite for the entire technology tree, unlocking [Agriculture]( {{< relref "techs/Agriculture.md" >}}) and the **Time tab**.

### **Functionality**
Once researched, the Calendar provides essential information about the passage of time in your village:
*   **Yearly Progression:** It allows you to see the current day and season.
*   **Village Age:** It displays the total number of years your civilization has existed.
*   **Time Tab:** It unlocks the dedicated Time tab, where late-game temporal mechanics (like Redshift and Chronoforges) are managed.

### **The Time System**
The game operates on a **tick system**, where one "tick" lasts 200 milliseconds. The units of time are defined as follows:
*   **Day:** 10 ticks (2 seconds of real-time).
*   **Season:** 100 days (3 minutes and 20 seconds).
*   **Year:** 400 days (13 minutes and 20 seconds).
*   **Cycle:** 5 years (1 hour, 6 minutes, and 40 seconds).
*   **Millennium:** 1,000 years (approximately 9 days and 6 hours).

### **Seasonal and Weather Mechanics**
The calendar year is divided into four seasons—**Spring, Summer, Autumn, and Winter**—which directly affect catnip production. 
*   **Catnip Production:** Fields receive a **+50% boost** in Spring and a **-75% penalty** in Winter. 
*   **Weather Modifiers:** Starting at **Year 4**, seasons have a 35% chance to be "warm" (improving catnip yields) or "cold" (decreasing them further). In a **Cold Winter**, catnip production can drop by **90%**.

### **Cycles and Late-Game Time**
The game features **ten five-year cycles** named after space destinations: **Charon, Umbra, Yarn, Helios, Cath, Redmoon, Dune, Piscine, Terminus (T-minus), and Kairo**.
*   **Looping:** The full sequence of cycles repeats every **50 years**.
*   **Modifiers:** With late-game [Metaphysics]( {{< relref "techs/Metaphysics.md" >}}) (Numerology and Numeromancy), these cycles provide various production boosts or penalties to space structures and festivals.
*   **Temporal Limits:** In the **1000 Years challenge**, the calendar stops advancing normally at year 500, requiring players to "shatter" Time Crystals to progress the year counter further.
