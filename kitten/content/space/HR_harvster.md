The **HR Harvester** (Hawking Radiation Harvester) is an advanced late-game space structure located at the black hole **Umbra**. It functions as a dynamic energy generator that operates on the energy of black hole evaporation.

### **Acquisition and Costs**
*   **Location:** Helios system, orbiting **Umbra**.
*   **Science/Mission Prerequisite:** Accessing Umbra requires a mission costing 25k Starcharts, 500k Science, 25k Kerosene, and 15k Thorium.
*   **Construction Cost:** **25 Relics** and **1,250 Antimatter**.
*   **Price Ratio:** 1.15.

### **Energy Production Mechanics**
The HR Harvester is unique because its power output is not static; it provides a base of **1 Watt** that increases indefinitely based on the age of your civilization and specific late-game upgrades.

*   **Dark Future Scaling:** The energy output is primarily driven by a "year bonus" that activates once the calendar passes **Year 40,000**. 
    *   The bonus increases based on the number of years passed beyond 40,000, including years gained by **shattering Time Crystals**.
    *   While the rate of increase slows down over time (following a square root formula), there is **no upper limit** to how high the energy production can go.
*   **Void Rift Synergy:** Each **Void Rift** (a structure in the Time tab) increases HR Harvester energy production by **10%**.

### **Environmental and Cycle Modifiers**
The effectiveness of the harvester fluctuates based on the current cosmic cycle if **Numerology** has been researched:
*   **Umbra Cycle:** Energy production is increased by **+50%**.
*   **Dune Cycle:** Energy production is reduced by **-25%**.

### **Strategic Importance**
*   **Energy Challenge:** Building at least one HR Harvester is a mandatory requirement for completing the **Energy Challenge**.
*   **Endgame Power Grid:** Because its production scales with years, it eventually becomes a vital source of power for energy-intensive endgame structures like **AI Cores** and **Entanglement Stations**, especially during "Deep Runs" where the year count reaches into the hundreds of thousands.
