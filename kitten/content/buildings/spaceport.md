The [Spaceport]( {{< relref "buildings/spaceport.md" >}}) is a high-tier late-game structure that serves as a direct upgrade to the [Warehouse]( {{< relref "buildings/Warehouse.md" >}}). It transitions your storage infrastructure from ground-based facilities into energy-consuming logistics hubs that significantly expand your capacity in space.

### **Acquisition and Costs**
*   **Prerequisite:** You must first research the **Exometallurgy** technology (formerly known as Advanced Exogeology).
*   **Upgrade Path:** It is an upgraded version of the Warehouse; however, if energy is lost, it can "downgrade" or revert back to its Warehouse state.
*   **Construction Costs:**
    *   [Titanium:]( {{< relref "resource/titanium.md" >}}) 10,000
    *   [Eludium:]( {{< relref "resource/eludium.md" >}}) 500
    *   [Kerosene:]( {{< relref "resource/kerosene.md" >}}) 1,000
    *   [Blueprints:]( {{< relref "resource/Blueprint.md" >}}) 500
    *   **Starcharts:** 400,000

### **Core Effects and Energy Consumption**
The Spaceport provides multiplicative storage bonuses to critical space structures rather than providing flat storage itself.
*   **Storage Buff:** Each Spaceport provides a **+0.85% bonus** to the storage capacity of:
    *   **Moon Bases**
    *   [Planet Crackers]( {{< relref "space/planet_cracker.md" >}})
    *   **Cryostations**
*   **Energy Consumption:** Unlike the standard Warehouse, which requires no power, each Spaceport consumes **5 Watts** of energy. 
*   **Scaling Drain:** The energy consumption of Spaceports begins to increase further once you have constructed **more than 10** of them.

### **Strategic Considerations**
The shift from Warehouses to Spaceports is a major infrastructure pivot. While the storage bonuses are essential for reaching the massive resource caps required for the deep endgame, the **-5W drain per building** (and higher beyond the 10th) can quickly overwhelm a mid-game energy grid. Players are generally advised to ensure their power production (from Sunlifters, Reactors, or Tectonics) is robust before committing to a mass upgrade of their Warehouse network.
