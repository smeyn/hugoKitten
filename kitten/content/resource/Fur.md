**Fur** is a rare luxury resource in *Kittens Game* that serves two primary functions: providing a **happiness boost** to your workforce and acting as the foundational material for **crafting parchment**.

### **Acquisition and Production**
There are two main methods to obtain fur:
*   **Hunting:** Once you research **Archery**, you can send your kittens on hunts. Each hunt costs **100 Catpower** and is guaranteed to yield some amount of fur. The amount returned is dictated by your **Hunting Bonus**, which is increased by workshop upgrades such as **Bolas**, **Hunting Armour**, **Steel Armour**, **Alloy Armour**, and **Nanosuits**.
*   **Mints:** In the mid-game, **Mints** (unlocked via Architecture) can be activated to automatically generate furs. The production rate of a Mint is tied to your **maximum Catpower capacity** (0.00875% of max catpower per tick). Using Mints is highly recommended during idle periods to stockpile fur without manual clicking.

### **Key Uses and Mechanics**
*   **Happiness and Efficiency:** As a "rare resource", fur provides a **+10% bonus to kitten happiness**. Because happy kittens are more productive, maintaining a supply of fur is a global production modifier. However, furs are **gradually consumed over time**, meaning you must constantly replenish them to maintain the bonus.
*   **Crafting Parchment:** Fur is the exclusive ingredient for **Parchment** (requiring 175 fur per craft). Parchment is an essential bottleneck resource used to create **Manuscripts**, which are required for nearly all mid-game scientific advancements and religious structures like **Temples**.
*   **Loss on Reset:** Like all luxury resources, accumulated furs are **lost upon resetting** the game.

### **Strategic Management and Policies**
The consumption and effectiveness of fur can be altered through various policies:
*   **Stoicism:** Reduces the consumption rate of luxury resources like fur by **50%**.
*   **Epicureanism:** Increases the happiness bonus provided by fur from 10% to **11%**.
*   **Extravagance:** Increases the happiness from consumable luxuries to 5 points but triples the consumption rate.
*   **Tradition:** While it doesn't affect fur directly, it reduces the amount of parchment needed for manuscripts, thereby making your fur-to-culture conversion more efficient.

**Note:** "Fur" is also one of the possible last names randomly assigned to your kittens upon their arrival in the village.
