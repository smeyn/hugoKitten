[Automated Plants]( {{< relref "upgrade/Automated_Plants.md" >}}) is a mid-to-late-game Workshop upgrade that enhances the passive steel production capabilities of your [Calciners]( {{< relref "buildings/Calciner.md" >}}).

### **Costs and Prerequisites**
To purchase this upgrade, you must have researched the [Robotics]( {{< relref "techs/Robotics.md" >}}) science technology.
*   **Science Cost:** 200,000
*   **Resource Cost:** 750 [Alloy]( {{< relref "craft/Alloy.md" >}})
*   **Prerequisite Upgrade:** You must first purchase [Steel Plants]( {{< relref "upgrade/Steel_Plants.md" >}}) in the Workshop.

### **Core Effect**
The primary function of Automated Plants is to scale the effectiveness of the [Steel Plants]( {{< relref "upgrade/Steel_Plants.md" >}}) upgrade using your village's industrial efficiency:
*   **Craft Ratio Bonus:** It applies **25% of your general craft ratio** as a bonus to the steel produced by Calciners.
*   **Resource Efficiency:** This bonus increases the steel yield **without increasing the consumption** of iron or coal.

### **Mechanical Context**
To understand the value of Automated Plants, it is helpful to look at the progression of Calciner steel upgrades:
1.  [Steel Plants:]( {{< relref "upgrade/Steel_Plants.md" >}}) This initial upgrade allows Calciners to automatically convert 10% of their iron output into steel (at a 100:1 ratio).
2.  [Automated Plants:]( {{< relref "upgrade/Automated_Plants.md" >}}) Leverages your Workshop and Factory bonuses to boost that output.
3.  [Nuclear Plants:]( {{< relref "upgrade/Nuclear_Plants.md" >}}) A subsequent upgrade (unlocked via Nuclear Fission) that further boosts this production by **2% per active Reactor**.

### **Strategic Utility**
Automated Plants is essential for transitioning away from manual steel crafting. While the "Theology hurdle" often requires manual clicking to get enough steel for Steamworks and early storage, this upgrade allows your industrial grid to produce significant quantities of steel passively. Because it scales with your **craft ratio**, it becomes increasingly powerful as you cap your [Workshops]( {{< relref "buildings/Workshop.md" >}}) (+6% each) and **Factories** (+5% each).
