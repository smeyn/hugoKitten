The [Alloy Axe]( {{< relref "upgrade/Alloy_Axe.md" >}}) is a late-mid-game Workshop upgrade that significantly improves the productivity of your [Woodcutter]( {{< relref "job/Woodcutter.md" >}}) kittens.

### **Costs and Prerequisites**
To unlock the Alloy Axe, you must first research the [Chemistry]( {{< relref "techs/Chemistry.md" >}}) science technology, which enables the production of [Alloy]( {{< relref "craft/Alloy.md" >}}). Once researched, the upgrade can be purchased in the Workshop for the following resources:
*   [Science:]( {{< relref "resource/science.md" >}}) 70,000
*   [Alloy:]( {{< relref "craft/Alloy.md" >}}) 25

### **Core Effect**
*   **Production Bonus:** Researching the Alloy Axe makes Woodcutters **50% more effective** (increasing your total `woodRatio`).
*   **Flavor Description:** The game describes these axes with the note: "The more you use them, the sharper they are!".

### **Strategic Context**
The Alloy Axe is part of a continuous chain of tool upgrades for your Woodcutters. It specifically follows the [Titanium Axe]( {{< relref "upgrade/Titanium_Axe.md" >}}) (+50%) and is eventually succeeded by the [Unobtainium Axe]( {{< relref "upgrade/Unobtainium_Axe.md" >}}) as you progress into deep-space operations. 

Because wood remains a foundational resource even in the late game—required for [Scaffolds]( {{< relref "craft/Scaffold.md" >}}), **Ships**, and various buildings—this 50% boost is essential for maintaining the high resource velocity needed to overcome late-game bottlenecks. While it provides the same percentage boost as the previous Titanium Axe, it is necessary to stack these additive bonuses to keep up with the escalating costs of advanced structures.
