The [Titanium Axe]( {{< relref "upgrade/Titanium_Axe.md" >}}) is a mid-to-late-game Workshop upgrade that provides a significant boost to your wood production infrastructure.

### **Costs and Prerequisites**
*   **Science Cost:** 38,000.
*   **Resource Cost:** 10 [Titanium]( {{< relref "resource/titanium.md" >}}).
*   **Prerequisite:** You must first research the [Navigation]( {{< relref "techs/Navigation.md" >}}) science technology.

### **Core Effects**
The Titanium Axe is described as being "indestructible" and provides a flat **+50% bonus to Woodcutter output** (woodRatio). It is part of a linear progression of woodcutting tools, following the Steel Axe and preceding the Alloy Axe.

### **Strategic Utility**
*   **Multiplicative Scaling:** A critical strategic detail of the axe upgrade chain is that its bonuses are **multiplicative with the production boost provided by Lumber Mills**. This makes the upgrade far more impactful than simple additive bonuses, allowing wood production to scale effectively as construction costs for advanced buildings like Harbours and Observatories rise.
*   **Transition into the Titanium Era:** Because this upgrade requires Titanium, it serves as one of the first practical uses for the resource after you begin trading with the [Zebras]( {{< relref "Civilisations/zebras.md" >}}). 
*   **Resolving Wood Bottlenecks:** Wood is often a major bottleneck in the mid-game for building Ships and Scaffolds; acquiring the Titanium Axe is essential for maintaining the velocity of your expansion during the "Titanium buildup" phase.
