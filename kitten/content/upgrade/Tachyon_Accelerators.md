[Tachyon Accelerators]( {{< relref "upgrade/Tachyon_Accelerators.md" >}}) is a powerful late-game Workshop upgrade that provides a massive boost to your resource storage capacity by enhancing the effectiveness of your [Accelerators]( {{< relref "buildings/Accelerator.md" >}}).

### **Prerequisites and Costs**
To unlock this upgrade, you must first research the [Tachyon Theory]( {{< relref "techs/Tachyon_Theory.md" >}}) science technology, which itself requires Chronophysics. 
*   **Upgrade Cost:** 500,000 Science, 125 Eludium, and 10 Time Crystals.

### **Core Effect: Energy Rift Enhancement**
The primary function of Tachyon Accelerators is to augment the [Energy Rifts]( {{< relref "upgrade/Energy_Rifts.md" >}}) upgrade, which allows Accelerators to store basic materials in pocket dimensions.
*   **Storage Multiplier:** It makes Energy Rifts **5 times as effective**, providing a flat **+500% bonus** to the maximum storage of **catnip, wood, minerals, coal, iron, titanium, and gold** provided by Accelerators.
*   **Additive Stacking:** This bonus stacks additively with other Accelerator storage upgrades, such as Stasis Chambers (+95%), Void Energy (+75%), and Dark Energy (+250%). When all upgrades are combined, the total storage bonus for Accelerators reaches **+920%**.

### **Strategic Utility**
Tachyon Accelerators are a cornerstone of late-game **resource scaling**. By the time you reach Tachyon Theory, basic storage buildings like Barns and Warehouses often hit their limits. This upgrade allows your industrial infrastructure to double as high-capacity storage, which is essential for:
*   **Bypassing Bottlenecks:** Reaching the massive titanium and gold caps required for advanced space structures and expensive religious upgrades.
*   **Endgame Resets:** Increasing your resource caps to maximize the amount of materials you can carry over when resetting with [Chronospheres]( {{< relref "buildings/Chronosphere.md" >}}).
*   **Science Scaling:** Helping you reach the high science caps required for endgame research like [Void Space]( {{< relref "techs/Void_Space.md" >}}) (800k) or [Paradox Theory]( {{< relref "techs/Paradox_Theory.md" >}}) (1M) by allowing you to store more resources used in crafting Compendiums.
