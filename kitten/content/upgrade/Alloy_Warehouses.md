[Alloy Warehouses]( {{< relref "upgrade/Alloy_Warehouses.md" >}}) is a mid-to-late-game Workshop upgrade that significantly boosts the storage capacity of your village's infrastructure.

### **Acquisition and Costs**
*   **Prerequisite Science:** You must first research [Chemistry]( {{< relref "techs/Chemistry.md" >}}), which requires Physics and Navigation.
*   **Resource Cost:**
    *   [Science:]( {{< relref "resource/science.md" >}}) 90,000
    *   [Titanium:]( {{< relref "resource/titanium.md" >}}) 750
    *   [Alloy:]( {{< relref "craft/Alloy.md" >}}) 50

### **Core Effects**
The upgrade provides a multiplicative storage bonus to several buildings:
*   **Storage Increase:** It increases the maximum amount of **wood, minerals, coal, iron, gold, and titanium** stored in **Warehouses, Barns, Harbours, and Mints** by **45%**.
*   **Gold Storage:** Specifically for Mints, this upgrade is a primary way to scale your maximum gold capacity, which is vital for late-game trading and religion.

### **Strategic Utility**
*   **The "Alloy Era":** Acquiring Alloy Warehouses and [Alloy Barns]( {{< relref "upgrade/Alloy_Barns.md" >}}) is a major milestone often reached when a player has between **90 and 100 kittens**. 
*   **Titanium Management:** Because this upgrade requires 750 Titanium, players must prioritize building a large fleet of [Trade Ships]( {{< relref "craft/Trade_Ship.md" >}}) and **Observatories** to ensure reliable Titanium yields from trading with [Zebras]( {{< relref "Civilisations/zebras.md" >}}).
*   **Progression Bottleneck:** Increasing your storage caps with Alloy-tier upgrades is often a mandatory step to afford the science costs of the **Concrete era**, specifically the 125,000 science limit required for [Concrete Huts]( {{< relref "upgrade/Concrete_Huts.md" >}}).
*   **Automation Synergy:** While Warehouses themselves do not initially store catnip, if you have researched [Silos]( {{< relref "upgrade/Silos.md" >}}), they gain catnip storage. However, unlike "Barn" upgrades, the Alloy Warehouses upgrade does not contribute to the additional 25% catnip storage bonus.

By expanding resource caps, Alloy Warehouses allow you to accumulate the massive quantities of materials needed for advanced industrial structures like **Factories**, [Reactors]( {{< relref "buildings/Reactor.md" >}}), and early space missions.
