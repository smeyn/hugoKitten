The [Steel Axe]( {{< relref "upgrade/Steel_Axe.md" >}}) is a mid-game Workshop upgrade that significantly increases your civilization's wood production efficiency. It is unlocked after researching the [Steel]( {{< relref "craft/Steel.md" >}}) science technology.

### **Cost and Requirements**
*   **Science Cost:** 20,000.
*   **Resource Cost:** 75 Steel.
*   **Prerequisite:** Researching the [Steel]( {{< relref "craft/Steel.md" >}}) technology is mandatory for this upgrade to appear in the Workshop.

### **Core Effects**
The Steel Axe is described as being "very sharp and durable" and provides a flat **+50% bonus to Woodcutter output** (woodRatio). This upgrade is part of a linear progression chain for Woodcutters, following the Mineral Axe (+70%) and Iron Axe (+50%) and preceding the Titanium Axe.

### **Strategic Utility**
The axe chain of workshop upgrades is particularly powerful because its bonuses are **multiplicative with the production boost from Lumber Mills**. Because wood production often becomes a major bottleneck for constructing advanced buildings like **Observatories** and [Harbours]( {{< relref "buildings/Harbour.md" >}}) in the mid-game, acquiring the Steel Axe is essential for maintaining infrastructure scaling once you have established a steady supply of steel.
