The [Steel Saw]( {{< relref "upgrade/Steel_Saw.md" >}}) is a mid-game **Workshop upgrade** designed to scale your civilization's wood production by improving the output of your [Lumber Mills]( {{< relref "buildings/Lumber_Mill.md" >}}).

### **Costs and Requirements**
*   **Science Cost:** 52,000.
*   **Resource Cost:** 750 Steel.
*   **Prerequisite:** You must research the [Physics]( {{< relref "techs/Physics.md" >}}) technology to unlock this upgrade in the Workshop.

### **Core Effects**
*   **Efficiency Boost:** It provides an additional **+2% wood production bonus** for every [Lumber Mill]( {{< relref "buildings/Lumber_Mill.md" >}}) you have built.
*   **Statistical Impact:** A base Lumber Mill provides +10% wood production; the Steel Saw raises this total to **12% per mill**. In practical terms, this makes building additional Lumber Mills more impactful, providing a relative wood production increase of approximately **20.69%** when moving from 40 to 50 mills (compared to only 20% without the upgrade).
*   **Scaling:** This bonus is part of the "Saw" upgrade chain, which is essential for overcoming the "slow wood accumulation" typically experienced in the mid-game.

### **Upgrade Progression**
The Steel Saw is the second tier in the specialized saw lineage:
1.  [Reinforced Saw:]( {{< relref "upgrade/Reinforced_Saw.md" >}}) +2% bonus (Requires Construction).
2.  [Steel Saw:]( {{< relref "upgrade/Steel_Saw.md" >}}) +2% bonus (Requires Physics).
3.  [Titanium Saw:]( {{< relref "upgrade/Titanium_Saw.md" >}}) +1.5% bonus (Requires Steel Saw).
4.  [Alloy Saw:]( {{< relref "upgrade/Alloy_Saw.md" >}}) +1.5% bonus (Requires Titanium Saw).

### **Strategic Utility**
At this stage of the game, wood production is often a major bottleneck for constructing **Observatories**, [Harbours]( {{< relref "buildings/Harbour.md" >}}), and [Trade Ships]( {{< relref "craft/Trade_Ship.md" >}}). While individual global multipliers (like Kittens or Magnetos) are generally preferred, the Saw upgrades are highly valued because they are multiplicative with the **Axe chain** of upgrades (Iron, Steel, Alloy, etc.), providing a necessary foundation for your industrial economy.
