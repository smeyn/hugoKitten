[Thin Film Cells]( {{< relref "upgrade/Thin_Film_Cells.md" >}}) is a specialized Workshop upgrade designed to enhance the reliability of your energy grid during the winter season by improving the efficiency of [Solar Farms]( {{< relref "buildings/Solar_Farm.md" >}}).

### **Costs and Prerequisites**
To unlock and purchase this upgrade, you must meet the following criteria:
*   **Technology:** You must first research the [Satellites]( {{< relref "techs/Satellites.md" >}}) science technology.
*   **Science Cost:** 125,000.
*   **Resource Cost:** 200 [Unobtainium]( {{< relref "resource/unobtainium.md" >}}) and 1,000 [Uranium]( {{< relref "resource/uranium.md" >}}).

### **Core Effect**
The primary function of Thin Film Cells is to mitigate the seasonal drop in solar energy production:
*   **Winter Boost:** It provides a **+15% bonus to Solar Farm energy production during Winter**.
*   **Context:** Without upgrades, Solar Farm output drops to its lowest level (+1.5 Watts) in Winter compared to Summer (+2.67 Watts). This upgrade helps stabilize your power levels when environmental conditions are least favourable.

### **Synergy and Stacking**
Thin Film Cells is part of a progression of solar upgrades:
*   [Quantum Dot Cells:]( {{< relref "upgrade/Quantum_Dot_Cells.md" >}}) This later upgrade (unlocked via Thorium) provides an additional +15% energy boost in winter that **stacks additively** with Thin Film Cells, for a total winter bonus of +30%.
*   [Photovoltaic Cells:]( {{< relref "upgrade/Photovoltaic_Cells.md" >}}) While Thin Film Cells targets winter specifically, Photovoltaic Cells (unlocked via Nanotechnology) provides a flat **+50% bonus** to Solar Farm production that multiplies with these seasonal effects.

### **Strategic Utility**
This upgrade is particularly useful in the **late-mid game** as you transition your infrastructure from Pastures to Solar Farms. Because energy-consuming structures like Calciners, Factories, and Satellites do not reduce their power demand in winter, having Thin Film Cells is vital for preventing a "negative energy" state that would penalize your global production.
